Tip #10

03/29/17

Study the behavioral dimension.

What people say and what people do are very different. When it comes to product design, rely on the behavior dimension rather than the attitudinal dimension by performing usability test, field studies, A/B testing, and card sorting.

3/28/17

Make on screen copy scannable.

Eye tracking shows us that users read in an F shaped pattern incredibly quickly on a screen. Make sure to split up your copy into scannable sections with proper line breaks, sub headers and bullet points.

Tip #7

3/26/17

Good design encourages efficient scanning.

Eye tracking shows us that how a user interacts with a page is dependent on their goals. When designing, think about every task that can be completed and design an interface that encourages scanning to complete those tasks.

Tip #6

3/25/17

The fold still matters.

Users treat info above the fold vs below with an 84% difference. Yes, the fold depends on screen sizes and people scroll, but they need to be enticed to do so. Give them a good reason to do so by telling a good story.
